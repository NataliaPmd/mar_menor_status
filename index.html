Chlorophyll (µg/L)

Chlorophyll indicates how many algae are present in the water. High values mean algae are proliferating, which can deplete oxygen and damage the ecosystem. The red line marks the risk threshold: 2 µg/L.

Dissolved Oxygen (mg/L)

Fish and marine life need dissolved oxygen to survive. Below 3 mg/L, suffocation events can occur and species begin to die off. The red line shows this critical limit.

Water Transparency (m)

The more transparent the water, the healthier the lagoon. When algae or suspended sediments accumulate, water becomes murky and sunlight cannot reach the bottom, harming seagrass.

Rambla del Albujón — Nitrates (mg/L)

Nitrates are the main nutrient that enters Mar Menor via the Rambla del Albujón. High nitrate concentrations after rainfall events feed algae blooms. Values are measured at sampling stations along the channel and averaged.
